Section 1: Business Problem Definition

A retail chain needs to identify optimal Chicago neighborhoods for new store locations by analyzing business license density to avoid oversaturated markets and target underserved areas. This data shows current business distribution, enabling identification of neighborhoods with balanced competition and customer potential.

Section 2: Data Source Assessment

Data Source Documentation

Direct URL: https://data.cityofchicago.org/Community-Economic-Development/Business-Licenses-Current-Active/u77p-8jgp
Organization: City of Chicago Department of Business Affairs & Consumer Protection
Original Purpose: Regulatory compliance for business licensing and tax collection
Time Period: Current active licenses, updated daily
Geographic Scope: All licensed businesses within Chicago city limits
Limitations: Some geographic fields have 8% missing data; SSA field has 74% missing

Access Method Implementation

# Use aggregate()
neighborhood_business <- aggregate(list(Count = business_data$ID), 
                                  by = list(Neighborhood = business_data$COMMUNITY.AREA.NAME), 
                                  FUN = length)

# Use group_by()  
license_types <- business_data %>% group_by(LICENSE.DESCRIPTION) %>% summarize(Count = n())

# Visualization 1: Business by neighborhood
ggplot(head(neighborhood_business, 10), aes(x = reorder(Neighborhood, Count), y = Count)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity") + coord_flip() +
  labs(title = "Businesses by Neighborhood", x = "Neighborhood", y = "Count")

# Visualization 2: License types
ggplot(head(license_types, 8), aes(x = reorder(LICENSE.DESCRIPTION, Count), y = Count)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity") + coord_flip() +
  labs(title = "Business License Types", x = "License Type", y = "Count")

# Visualization 3: Scatter Plot - Total Businesses vs Restaurants
restaurant_data <- business_data %>% filter(grepl("Food", BUSINESS.ACTIVITY))
restaurant_summary <- restaurant_data %>% group_by(COMMUNITY.AREA.NAME) %>% summarize(Restaurants = n())

scatter_data <- neighborhood_business %>%
  rename(Total_Businesses = Count) %>%
  left_join(restaurant_summary, by = c("Neighborhood" = "COMMUNITY.AREA.NAME"))

ggplot(scatter_data, aes(x = Total_Businesses, y = Restaurants)) +
  geom_point() +
  geom_smooth(method = "lm", se = FALSE) +
  labs(title = "Relationship: Total Businesses vs. Restaurants by Neighborhood",
       x = "Total Number of Businesses",
       y = "Number of Restaurants")
## `geom_smooth()` using formula

Chart 1 shows business density. Chart 2 reveals license distribution. Chart 3 shows the correlation between total businesses and restaurant concentration. All inform location strategy by visualizing market patterns.

Pattern Recognition

  1. Geographic: Near North Side (4,587 businesses) has 4x more businesses than Ashburn (1,112), indicating significant market density variation.
  2. Category: Limited Business Licenses (15,476) dominate over Retail Food Establishments (3,456), showing small business environment favors new entrants.

Section 4: Strategic Recommendations

  1. Avoid Near North Side and Loop (7,000+ businesses) due to saturation.
  2. Target moderate-density areas like Portage Park (2,100 businesses) for balanced competition.
